01 — About

Reading landscapes
through data.

I'm an MS student in Geographic Information Science in the Department of Geography and the Environment at the University of Denver, expected to graduate in August 2027. My thesis examines the gap between spatial accessibility and actual travel behavior in U.S. cities — identifying where "15-minute city" conditions exist theoretically but not in practice — using OSMnx for street-network construction and network-based service area delineation.

Alongside my thesis, I work as a Graduate Teaching Assistant under Dr. Steven Hick, leading weekly labs of about 25 students in ArcGIS Pro and ArcPy. Before Denver, I spent two and a half years as a Research Consultant in Energy & Infrastructure Geospatial Analytics at the Center for Environmental and Geographic Information Services (CEGIS) in Dhaka, building end-to-end geospatial pipelines — multispectral satellite imagery, UAV data, and vector datasets — for infrastructure feasibility and environmental impact assessments. I hold a Bachelor of Urban and Regional Planning from Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (KUET).

  • Remote sensing & GeoAI

    Building on my CEGIS work in automated land-use/land-cover classification from satellite and UAV imagery, I use Google Earth Engine for spectral index computation and deep learning (CNN/ResNet50) for image classification — most recently applied to medical imaging as a methods transfer exercise, and to environmental/land-cover analysis in ongoing coursework.

  • Reproducible geospatial data engineering

    A recurring thread across my projects is building pipelines, not one-off maps: relational geodatabase design (ER diagrams, topology, subtypes), GDAL/OGR raster-vector workflows, and parallelized batch processing of multi-tile DEM data that mirrors HPC job-array patterns.

Custom ArcPy geoprocessing tool in ArcGIS Pro that quantifies and ranks neighborhood-level accessibility to essential services — schools, parks, food retail, transit, healthcare — across Denver Metro, using weighted dual scoring of service density and spatial proximity over a reproducible, configurable pipeline.

ArcPy · ArcGIS Pro · Final Project, Python Programming in GIS

NATIONWIDE · NHTS 2022 · NOV 2025

Origin–Destination Accessibility Analysis

Designed a relational GIS geodatabase linking NextGen NHTS 2022 origin–destination travel data with spatial zone boundaries — ER diagrams, topology rules, domains, subtypes — then analyzed commuting patterns, mode shares, and X-minute city accessibility across the contiguous U.S. via SQL and cartographic mapping.

ArcGIS Pro · SQL · Final Project, GIS Database Design
