01 — About

Reading landscapes
through data.

I'm a graduate teaching assistant and researcher at the University of Denver, working at the intersection of Geographic Information Systems, remote sensing, and urban & transportation planning. My work moves between satellite imagery, LiDAR, and drone-collected data on one end, and the everyday realities of roads, land use, and disaster risk on the other.

Before Denver, I worked as a GIS & Remote Sensing Research Consultant at the Center for Environmental and Geographic Information Services (CEGIS) in Bangladesh, where I supported feasibility studies for infrastructure projects — including environmental and social impact assessments, land use mapping, and field surveys. I hold a bachelor's degree in Urban and Regional Planning from Khulna University of Engineering & Technology (KUET).

03 — Projects

Selected work.

22.8°N, 89.5°E · KHULNA, BD

Ecosystem Services Change Detection, Khulna City

Tracked ecosystem service and disservice change across two urban wards over a 15-year span (2005–2020) using multispectral satellite imagery. Combined NDVI and NDBI indices with DEM-based service area analysis to map how urban growth reshaped riverside ecology.

GIS · Remote Sensing · NDVI/NDBI · DEM

FEASIBILITY STUDY · ON-SHORE GAS PIPELINE

Geospatial Impact Assessment, CEGIS

Applied GIS and remote sensing — including Google Earth Engine — to conduct environmental and social impact assessments for an on-shore gas pipeline feasibility study. Produced land-use maps from satellite, LiDAR, and drone data, and coordinated field surveys including route and bathymetric surveys.

Google Earth Engine · LiDAR · Drone Survey

WARD-BASED · TRAFFIC & TRANSPORT PLANNING

Traffic & Transport Management Plan

Analyzed ward-based traffic composition, road network conditions, parking, and pedestrian infrastructure to propose improvements for motorized and non-motorized networks — with an emphasis on pedestrian movement and control.

Network Analysis · Field Survey
